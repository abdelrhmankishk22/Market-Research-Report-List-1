<p><p>The hydrogen fuel storage system market can be classified into four types - Type I, Type II, Type III, and Type IV. Type I refers to hydrogen storage in high-pressure cylindrical tanks made of steel or aluminum. Type II involves storing hydrogen at a higher pressure in reinforced composite material tanks. Type III involves hydrogen storage in high-pressure cylinders with a lightweight liner wrapped in carbon fibers. Type IV is about storing hydrogen at a lower pressure in tanks made of lightweight materials with a non-metallic liner and reinforced with carbon fibers.</p></p>
