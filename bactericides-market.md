<p><p>The bactericides market encompasses various types such as active chlorine, iodine, concentrated alcohols, phenolic substances, and cationic surfactants. Active chlorine-based bactericides are effective in killing bacteria by oxidizing their proteins and other vital components. Iodine-based bactericides work by disrupting the cell membranes of bacteria. Concentrated alcohols act as bactericides by denaturing proteins and disrupting cell membranes. Phenolic substances have a wide spectrum of activity against various bacteria by denaturing proteins and interfering with their metabolic processes. Cationic surfactants kill bacteria by disrupting their cell membranes through electrostatic interactions.</p></p>
