<p><p>The artificial preservative market includes various types such as sorbates, benzoates, propionates, nitrites, sulfates, and others. Sorbates, like potassium sorbate, are used to inhibit fungal and microbial growth in food and beverages. Benzoates, such as sodium benzoate, prevent the growth of bacteria and yeast by acidifying the environment. Propionates, including calcium propionate, are effective against mold and certain bacteria. Nitrites are commonly used in cured meats to inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria. Sulfates, like sodium sulfite, act as antioxidants and antimicrobials. Other artificial preservatives encompass a wide range of substances with various preservation properties.</p></p>
