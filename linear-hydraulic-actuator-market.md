<p><p>The linear hydraulic actuator market encompasses various types, such as single-acting, double-acting, double-rod, rodless, and others. Single-acting actuators use hydraulic pressure to generate force in one direction and rely on external forces to return to their original position. Double-acting actuators can produce force in both directions and are commonly used for applications requiring precise control. Double-rod actuators have piston rods on both ends, providing enhanced stability and balanced force transmission. Rodless actuators use a piston-mounted carriage system to achieve linear motion, making them suitable for limited space applications. Other market segments may include specialized or advanced actuator types.</p></p>
