<p><p>The non-woven polypropylene fiber market is categorized into three types based on the melt flow rate (MFR) of the material. MFR is a measure of a polymer's viscosity and indicates the ease of extrusion during the manufacturing process. The three types are defined by their MFR values: </p><p>1) < 25 g/10 min: This category includes fibers with lower MFR, indicating a slower extrusion rate. These fibers are generally used in applications that require high tensile strength and stiffness, such as geotextiles or automotive components.</p><p>2) 25-40 g/10 min: This category encompasses fibers with a moderate MFR. They are commonly utilized in applications like hygiene products, filtration media, or disposable non-woven fabrics.</p><p>3) >40 g/10 min: This category includes fibers with higher MFR, indicating a faster extrusion rate. These fibers are often used in non-woven fabrics for industrial purposes like geotextiles, carpet backings, or specialty packaging materials.</p></p>
