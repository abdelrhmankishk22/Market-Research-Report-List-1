<p><p>In the infrared combustible gas sensors market, there are two main types of detectors available: point detectors and open path detectors. </p><p>Point detectors are compact devices designed to detect the presence of combustible gases at a specific point or area. These detectors are commonly used in residential and industrial settings to provide localized gas detection and alert users to potential hazards. </p><p>Open path detectors, on the other hand, use infrared beams to monitor a larger area for the presence of combustible gases. They consist of a transmitter and a receiver, placed opposite to each other, and can detect gas leaks over long distances. These detectors are commonly used in outdoor environments or large industrial sites where gas leak detection across a wide area is necessary.</p></p>
