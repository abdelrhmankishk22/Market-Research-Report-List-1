<p><p>The Universal I/O Module market encompasses two main types: digital and analog. Digital modules are designed to process discrete signals in the form of on/off states, with signals represented by binary digits (bits). These modules are commonly used in applications that require simple input/output operations, such as button presses or switch actions. On the other hand, analog modules are designed to handle continuous signals with varying voltage or current levels. They are used for measuring and processing physical quantities like temperature, pressure, or voltage, requiring greater precision and accuracy compared to digital modules.</p></p>
